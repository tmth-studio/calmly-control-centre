What is IVE?

The methodology that Calmly Resolve was built with — and the product opportunity for venture 2.

The Integrated Venture Engine is a structured methodology for evaluating and building ventures. It was developed through the Tough Minds Tender Hearts system and proved out on Calmly Resolve — taking a concept from zero to CDR-passed and Phase 5 in a compressed timeline.

The methodology has three interlocking components. Together they form a repeatable system for building ventures with high architectural discipline and low wasted capital.

A Venture architecture

CDR, BALM, phase gates, systems engineering (V-model), and the slice-based build sequence. The framework that takes a concept to a TRR-ready build.

B Investment evaluation

Financial simulation (FMOS), scenario modelling, capital gate logic, and the financial due diligence that backs every phase decision.

C Founder and team assessment

The role architecture, decision authority, and operating principles that govern how the venture runs — including the autonomous-first dispatch model.

The PCO gate — the real first step

Forge is built using IVE itself. The first gate is the PCO — the IVE scope-setter — not generic product strategy. Nothing is architected until it's confirmed.

The broadest possible use case for a core functionality that can most credibly generate the at-scale cash flows needed to pay back all required investment capital at a competitive rate of return. Scope before depth.

The PCO deliberately keeps the solution space open. It does not ask "what's the product / wedge / V1" — bounding problem and solution together, or anchoring on a customer segment, are the two failures the PCO exists to prevent.

1
Investment parameters

Period, target IRR, at-scale geography, industry. These bound what "credibly generates at-scale cash flows" means and become the screen every use case is tested against.

2
Abstract the core functionality

What the IVE engine does and how — stripped of all customer context. Forge is a Path A venture: the capability already exists, so use cases are applications of it.

3
Generate use cases (≥6)

Broad applications of the functionality at the level of the job to be done — not customer segments. Two people in one segment may have different use cases; two in different segments may share one.

4
Evaluate against the financial screen

For each use case: is the group large enough, is the customer loss large enough, is a credible workaround possible. Loss-to-price ratio above 2x is a viable direction.

5
State the PCO

One sentence: who + the condition they face + why the financial shape works. This is the investor-level confirmation every downstream requirement traces to.

Next step

One action unlocks everything else.

Required before any work begins

Run the PCO. Open /ceo-forge — the CEO confirms the path (A or B), then runs /balm-pco-custom to produce the PCO statement for investor confirmation. Nothing downstream — no requirement, no model, no product choice — is built until the PCO is confirmed in writing.
