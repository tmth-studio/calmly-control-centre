Calmly Resolve — Experience Prototype Screen Inventory

8 digital products across 5 build slices. Each screen annotated with: (1) CTM flow reference, (2) T4 CDM assumption under test, (3) pass/fail validation criterion. Prototypes are validation instruments — every screen either confirms or falsifies an architecture assumption. 28 May 2026.

Architectural constraint — applies to every screen. Each prototype component must do one of two things: (a) confirm an architecture assumption (pass criterion met), or (b) surface a falsifying signal (fail criterion met). A screen that does neither is decorative and should not be built. The T4 assumption column is mandatory — if no assumption can be named, the screen needs a clearer purpose or the prototype scope is too broad.

This is the ITP's submission, not the claimant's self-submission — the ITP vouches for the member. Dispute type must map directly to CDM segments (S-001 through S-004). Tells the ITP when they will receive an outcome update for this member (ties to O-001 specialist capacity). Gives ITP a referral reference number — they can share it with the member. Fail: ITP says "I wouldn't use this — too slow / too unclear / doesn't fit our workflow."
P3-S4 Referral history / status list List of all referrals submitted by this ITP, with current status (submitted / under assessment / EV complete / settlement offer sent / resolved). This is the primary C8 switching cost mechanism — the ITP's outcome history is visible here and nowhere else. P2-S1 Case status view Shows current position in the CDR process: submitted → under assessment → EV complete → settlement offer sent → resolved. Queue position in pool (e.g. "37 of 142 active pool members"). Pool size is the collective threat signal — larger pool = stronger collective threat = better defendant incentive to settle. This must be legible to a non-legal claimant in under 30 seconds. CP1 WP1 O-001 Module capacity Pass: Claimant can state their current status correctly after 15 seconds on this screen. Fail: Claimant asks "what stage am I at?" despite being on the status screen.
P2-S2 Pool position overview Shows the claimant's pool position: collective threat share (their contribution to the aggregated claim value that the defendant faces), insurance status (protected against loss up to X during assessment), and queue position. This is the C4 / C8 hedged long position — must make the three-dimension lock-in visible without explaining the theory. Claimant must feel "I have a position I'd lose if I walked away." WP1 C4 C8-CLM M-003 Claimant persistence M-006 Pool withdrawal rate Pass: Claimant expresses reluctance to withdraw when shown their pool position (qualitative interview). Fail: Claimant says "I don't know what this means" or "it doesn't change my thinking."
P2-S3 EV report presentation Presents the independently endorsed expected value assessment: EV range (low / central / high), confidence level, comparison to likely judgment value, and the escalation schedule (what the defendant faces at each stage of legal escalation). Must resolve the Key Learning Block (C5): "I have no way to know if this offer is fair." The word "independently endorsed" must be explained — claimant must understand this is not Calmly's own estimate. WP2 C5 M-005 Avg settlement £3,000 M-007 EV accuracy Pass: Claimant says "I now understand what my claim is worth" after viewing the EV report. Fail: Claimant says "I'd still need a solicitor to tell me if this is right" — the independent endorsement is not credible to them.
P2-S4 Settlement offer screen Presents the defendant's settlement offer (once received), the EV report as a reference point, and three options: accept / counter / proceed to escalation. Must make the fee calculation transparent: "If you accept £2,800, Calmly's fee is £560 (20%), your net recovery is £2,240." The contingent fee deduction is the C6 payment event — architecturally inseparable from value delivery. Claimant should feel the fee is fair, not extractive. CP2 C6 U-002 Fee rate 20% M-005 Avg settlement Pass: Claimant understands fee deduction and does not report surprise at the net amount. Fail: Claimant says "I didn't know you'd take 20%" — fee was not visible or not legible at intake.
P2-S5 Recovery confirmation + fee breakdown Final screen: settlement accepted, fee deducted, net recovery to be disbursed. Itemised: gross settlement, platform fee (%), net to claimant, disbursement timeline. This is the Repeating phase trigger (C8-CLM) — a claimant who successfully resolved will refer others if satisfied. The outcome screen must be designed to generate a referral or ITP endorsement, not just to confirm a number. CP3 C6 U-002 Fee rate 20% U-004 FMOS Pass: Claimant NPS ≥ 8 post-resolution. Fail: Claimant NPS < 6 — either outcome was below expectation, fee felt extractive, or process took too long.

P7-S1 Case queue All active cases assigned to this specialist. Columns: case reference, ITP source, dispute type, claim value, date received, current status, days in queue. Priority ordering: days in queue (oldest first). This screen is the primary O-001 test surface — if a specialist is handling 40 cases/month, their queue at any point should show a manageable pipeline without cases ageing beyond the SLA. INT-Q1 O-001 Specialist capacity 40/month — CRITICAL Pass: Specialist says queue reflects their actual workload — no cases ageing beyond 10 working days. Fail: Cases routinely age >10 days — O-001 assumption (40/month) is too aggressive for real processing time.
P7-S2 Case detail view Full intake data for one case: claimant details, ITP source, dispute narrative, estimated value, segment classification (CDM S-001 through S-004). Specialist uses this to validate the intake, confirm segment, and begin EV methodology. If intake is incomplete, specialist can flag for follow-up (triggering email to claimant). Must not require specialist to leave this screen to access segment-specific EV methodology guidance. INT-Q2 O-001 Capacity M-004 Segment composition Pass: Specialist can determine segment and begin EV methodology without navigating away. Fail: Specialist needs to consult a separate document or spreadsheet to complete assessment — the tool does not have the methodology embedded.
P7-S3 EV assessment workflow Guided EV assessment: inputs the specialist provides (dispute type, claim value, evidence quality, defendant type, jurisdiction), the methodology calculates the EV range (low/central/high), and the specialist reviews and confirms (or adjusts with documented rationale). The output is submitted for independent endorsement. This is C5 in operation — the working product that produces the endorsed EV report the claimant sees in P2-S3. WP2 C5 M-005 Avg settlement £3,000 M-007 EV accuracy Pass: Specialist completes EV assessment in <45 minutes per case at standard complexity. Fail: Assessment takes >2 hours — O-001 capacity assumption is broken before endorsement step is even reached.
P7-S4 Case routing / status update After assessment complete: specialist routes case to next stage (EV sent for endorsement / defendant demand generated / flagged for review). Status update automatically flows through to claimant P2-S1 (status) and ITP P3-S4 (referral history). This is the operational integration point — the specialist's action triggers downstream screen updates across all actor-facing products. INT-Q3 O-001 Capacity Pass: Specialist status update reflects in claimant and ITP portals within 60 seconds. Fail: Status lag creates claimant or ITP queries — real-time sync is not working.
⚠ O-001 is the highest-risk T4 assumption. The 40 cases/month specialist capacity is a modelled estimate with no operational data behind it. Product 7 is the only prototype that directly tests it. If the workflow in P7-S3 (EV assessment) takes materially longer than 45 minutes per case, O-001 breaks and the unit economics model needs revision before any other assumption can be trusted. Build this first within Slice 2.

P5-S1 Demand notice + EV report Presents the pooled claim demand: total pool value, individual claim component, EV report (independently endorsed), and the escalation schedule (what the defendant faces at D2, D3, court). The pool size is the collective threat made legible to the defendant — they are not facing one claimant, they are facing N claimants. The EV report must be presented as a credible, third-party-endorsed figure, not as Calmly's negotiating position. CP3 D1 C3 M-005 Avg settlement £3,000 M-007 EV credibility Pass: Defendant can articulate the EV figure, what endorses it, and what escalation means financially. Fail: Defendant dismisses EV as "Calmly's own estimate" — independence of endorsement is not credible on screen.
P5-S2 Liability buyout offer The buyout operator's fixed-price instrument: defendant can extinguish their entire liability at a fixed cost, at D1, with no further action required. Must make the financial comparison legible: buyout price vs estimated litigation cost vs EV if taken to judgment. This is C6 (payment product — D-side): the payment event for the defendant is the buyout acceptance, at which point the buyout operator assumes the liability. The defendant's response data (accept / negotiate / ignore) enriches the buyout operator's pricing model (C6 flywheel). D1 C6 U-002 Fee rate U-001 Cost floor £258 Pass: Defendant can identify the buyout price and compare it to the EV figure in <60 seconds. Fail: Defendant says "I don't understand what I'm agreeing to" — buyout instrument is not explained clearly enough for a lay defendant.
P5-S3 Response options Three defendant paths: (1) accept buyout, (2) make a counter-offer, (3) dispute / proceed to next escalation stage. The counter-offer path generates structured negotiation data — not a free-text field. The ignore/dispute path triggers the D2 escalation clock. All three responses must be captured for the buyout operator's pricing model (C6 flywheel) — the dataset of defendant responses at D1 is itself a switching cost asset for the buyout operator (C8, Track C). D1 C6 M-008 Defendant settlement rate Pass: All three defendant response paths are captured in structured data. Fail: Defendants default to "ignore" because the response interface is too complex — D1 response rate <30%.

P8-S1 Marketplace access portal Role-differentiated login: pool insurer sees their actuarial data and live claimant pool positions; buyout operator sees their live defendant positions and EV portfolio. Both roles share access to the endorsed EV portfolio — this is the shared underwriting basis (C6 exchange table, Section E). The role separation is the independence constraint in operation: pool insurer and buyout operator must not see each other's pricing or positions. SMP-P1 C8-SMP U-005 Marketplace access fee U-004 FMOS Pass: Pool insurer and buyout operator each confirm their data is role-appropriate and independent. Fail: Either party can see the other's pricing or positions — independence constraint is violated.
P8-S2 EV portfolio view The endorsed EV portfolio: all active cases with EV assessments, by segment, dispute type, and confidence level. This is the shared underwriting basis. Pool insurer uses it to price loss protection instruments; buyout operator uses it to price liability buyout instruments. The portfolio grows with claims volume — the D&C time compression diseconomy activates here (the calibration dataset cannot be replicated faster than real-time claims accumulation). View must show portfolio vintage and calibration accuracy trend over time. SMP-P2 C8-SMP M-007 EV accuracy U-005 Marketplace access fee Pass: SMP actuary can use EV portfolio data directly in their pricing model without data transformation. Fail: SMP actuary says "we'd need to reformat this before we could use it" — portfolio format does not map to underwriting models.
P8-S3 Live position / underwriting view Pool insurer: live claimant pool positions under their insurance instrument — number of insured claimants, aggregate exposure, expected loss rate, premium income. Buyout operator: live defendant positions under their buyout instrument — number of active positions, aggregate liability, expected acceptance rate, premium income. Both instruments fire marketplace access fees to Calmly independently of settlement timing — this is the second revenue stream. Must show the fee accrual visibly: "£X in marketplace access fees generated this period." SMP-P3 C6 U-005 Marketplace access fee — UNTESTED Pass: SMP can confirm their live position count, aggregate exposure, and marketplace access fee for the period in <30 seconds. Fail: SMP says "we'd need to download this and calculate it ourselves" — portal is not operationally useful.
P8-S4 Calibration trend / switching cost visibility The D&C asset accumulation made visible: EV accuracy trend over time (how the endorsed EV model has improved as claims data has accumulated), the SMP's own position portfolio size and vintage, and the implied recalibration cost if the SMP were to start on a competitor platform. This is the causal ambiguity reversal: typically the switching cost is invisible until after the switch. This screen makes it visible before — turning a latent lock-in into a salient one. This is the C8 Track C retention mechanism. C8-SMP U-005 Marketplace access fee M-007 EV accuracy Pass: SMP actuary says "moving platforms would set our model back — I can see that here." Fail: SMP says "this doesn't affect our decision to stay" — causal ambiguity is not resolved by the data shown.
⚠ U-005 (marketplace access fee rate) is the only T4 assumption not yet defined. The financial model has not yet set the fee rate for secondary market participants. Product 8 screens show the fee accruing — but the rate is currently a placeholder. This must be resolved in the financial model before Product 8 can be used in a real SMP conversation. Prototype can test the mechanic; it cannot test the pricing.
