Launch Components Map

Sibling document to the at-scale components map. Where the at-scale map describes the operating system in steady state, this map describes the foundation work to get there — the regulatory position, entity stack, capital, founding partnerships, trust foundation, operating infrastructure, and governance that must exist before any at-scale slice can run. Launch components are completed once and maintained; they do not scale with claim volume.

PCO: Commercial fast track — UK SME disputes £10k–£50k
Derived from: Business architecture · ARM constraints · Fin Sim Stage 1 gate
Version: v1

What this document is

The AOM, ARM and Fin Sim are at-scale documents — they model the steady-state operating system. They assume regulatory authorisation, capital, entity stack, founding partnerships and governance exist. This map inventories the work those assumptions hide. Each launch component is a state to be reached, not an activity to be performed repeatedly. Done once, maintained thereafter. Most launch work is founder-led with external advisor support; some hands to the Head of Legal & Operations as the venture matures. Cross-referenced to the at-scale slices each component unblocks.

How to read the detail cards

Each component below carries the activities required to reach the state, the requirements for "done", the owner(s), and the at-scale slice(s) it unblocks. Activities are mostly one-off (incorporate, sign, draft, secure) rather than recurring. The "requirements" column captures conditions for legitimate completion drawn from the existing modelling — LASPO floor from ARM, capital floor from Fin Sim Model 2 Stage 1, partnership thresholds from Fin Sim gates, Pool Operator integration from AOM.

Component detail — launch components

REG1 SRA position on claim assignment Regulatory · One-off + maintained
State to reach: Calmly's claim-purchase model has a confirmed, defensible SRA position. Either authorised activity, exempt activity, or structured so that the regulated parts sit with the Qualified Supervisor and external counsel.
Activities required
  • Engage specialist SRA counsel to map Calmly's model against SRA Handbook
  • Identify the regulated activities in WP1–WP6 and confirm responsibility chain
  • Secure written position from counsel; consider direct SRA engagement if material ambiguity
  • Draft Calmly's regulatory operating manual: what's done by whom, with what supervision
  • Build the audit and record-keeping that demonstrates ongoing compliance
Requirements derived from modelling
LASPO floor (ARM)

Qualified Supervisor sign-off on WP3 is a hard regulatory requirement. The SRA position must permit the student-draft / supervisor-signoff model that the ARM assumes.

Claim assignment (WP2)

The legal transfer in WP2 is what makes Calmly the party-in-interest. SRA position must confirm Calmly can hold assigned claims and act on them.

Cost-floor implication

£258 cost floor (Fin Sim v4) assumes a specific regulatory model. A different SRA outcome could change the cost floor materially.

Maintenance

Position must be reviewable against SRA Handbook changes. Annual review owned by Head of Legal & Ops at scale.

REG2 LASPO compliance framework Regulatory · One-off + maintained
State to reach: Documented LASPO compliance framework covering Qualified Supervisor sign-off (WP3), scope-of-service boundaries, and escalation rules. Trains the at-scale operating roles in what they can and cannot do.
Activities required
  • Document the Qualified Supervisor sign-off process for WP3, including criteria, format, audit
  • Define scope-of-service boundaries: what Calmly does, what it doesn't, what triggers referral to external counsel
  • Document escalation rules: when court counsel must be engaged (WP6), at what threshold
  • Build templates and decision trees that operating roles can follow without legal training
  • Train initial Qualified Supervisor; pilot framework on first cohort of cases
Requirements derived from modelling
ARM Constraint 1

LASPO floor = 0.5 FTE Qualified Supervisor minimum. Framework must accommodate the minimum-volume case.

Student-supervisor ratio

1 supervisor per 10–15 active students. Framework must specify supervision quality standards, not just sign-off.

Court escalation

~25% of cases (Fin Sim). Framework must say who decides, at what cost threshold, with what authorisation.

Pilot-then-scale

First cohort under the framework is also the proof. Quality patterns flagged to Claims Manager (per ARM).

REG3 FCA + AML + GDPR position + PI cover Regulatory · One-off + maintained
State to reach: Written opinions on FCA, AML and GDPR positions; data practices documented; professional indemnity cover in place sized to Stage 1 risk.
Activities required

PI sizing

Stage 1 claim values are £10–50k. PI cover must size to claim-value × volume × risk multiplier, not generic professional cover.

PART1 First ITP partnership Founding partnership · One-off (template)
State to reach: One ITP partnership signed. Referral mechanism live (defines how the ITP sends a claimant to CP1). Data-sharing terms agreed (defines how PartP1 outcomes flow back). This first deal is the template for the next 14+.
Activities required
  • Identify and engage candidate ITP partners with relevant claimant flow
  • Articulate the value exchange: claim flow into Calmly, outcomes data back to ITP, joint credibility
  • Negotiate referral mechanism, fees, data-sharing terms
  • Sign partnership agreement; integrate into CP1 inbound and PartP1 outbound
  • Document the deal structure for replication at Stage 2 (15+ partnerships)
Requirements derived from modelling
C7 channel mix (Fin Sim)

60% of CAC blend at scale comes from ITP referrals at £25 NTP fee. First partnership must validate this assumption is achievable.

C8 lock-in (AOM)

PartP1 outcomes report builds switching cost via data dependency. First partnership must include data flowback to seed C8.

Concentration limit

By Stage 2→3, no single ITP >30% of claim supply (PartP1 requirement). First partnership term must not lock Calmly out of broader market.

Template requirement

Deal terms must be replicable to 3+ at Stage 2 and 15+ at scale. Bespoke clauses hard to repeat are red flags.

What this view exposes

The founder is the single highest-touch launch owner (7 components). Head of Legal & Ops carries 3 components but is on the critical path for regulatory work that gates everything. Operations & Finance carries the data spine that future reporting depends on. External advisor spend is significant in launch and largely invisible in the at-scale unit economics — worth modelling separately.
