What is systems engineering — and why IVE?

Systems engineering is a structured, interdisciplinary development process for planning, designing, implementing, and validating a system. It front-loads definition work so that build and validation decisions are made against a verified architecture — not iteratively corrected after the fact. IVE applies this discipline specifically to new venture architectures, where the core risk is not technical failure but commercial architecture failure: the venture works operationally but cannot sustain a market position or pay back capital.

IVE — three levels

IVE operates at the level of the Core Business Architecture (CBA) — the logic that sets both the cost curve and the value curve for an industry. Below this sits the Business Form Factor (BFF) — the essential shape the product and operations take given a CBA. Below that sits the Business Model — the company's unique strategy for outcompeting others within a shared BFF. IVE intervenes at the CBA/BFF level. Business model decisions follow from a verified BFF, not precede it.

The IVE methodology decomposes CBA/BFF design into ten requirements (the BALM), organised across three functions: neutralise the value barrier (F1), normalise the customer routine (F2), and lock in the market position (F3). Each requirement is designed, theorised, and verified before the next begins. The output is a fully specified, internally consistent Business Form Factor — traceable from each requirement to every operational component.

The V-model — Calmly's process trajectory

The V-model maps IVE's design-build-validate cycle. The left side decomposes requirements into progressively more detailed design specifications. The base is the build point — where components are constructed. The right side integrates and validates components against the original requirements. Verification plans created during decomposition govern what is tested on the right side.

Decision gates

Each gate controls progression between phases. A gate passes when its governing question is fully answered with documented, defensible evidence. The gate sequence is load-bearing — CDR-quality design on an incomplete PDR produces the wrong system.

✓ PCOR PCO Review Is this the right problem, for the right customer, at a scale that can pay back capital?
✓ AR Architecture Review Is there a plausible F1 architecture with positive FMOS headroom?
✓ PDR Preliminary Design Review Are all 10 BALM requirements designed, FMOS ≥60%, and consistency audit PASS?
✓ CDR Critical Design Review Are requirements decomposed to components, RTM complete, interfaces defined, build sequence agreed?
→ TRR Test Readiness Review Are individual components verified and the workflow integration test ready to run?
· ORR Operational Readiness Review Has the MRP validated unit economics and does NPV exceed the hurdle rate?

The 10 BALM requirements

The BALM decomposes the venture's core business architecture into ten requirements across three functions. Each requirement is designed to a specific IVE standard and carries a named Theory of Change. The cumulative BFF statement after each function represents the current architecture in full.

# Function Requirement Calmly solution Status
R1 F1 Circumvent at-scale cost bottleneckWhat is the conventional operational model, and how is the CLO circumvented? Litigation pool replaces adversarial bilateral model. Assessment under proprietary EV methodology, independently endorsed. Spence (1973) signalling mechanism. ✓ Complete
R2 F1 Eliminate customer's biggest monetizable costWhat is the KMC? How does the product eliminate it? KMC: financial loss + certainty of outcome. Pool insurance removes the fear of early loss. Attendant routine: join → contribute → pursue collectively. ✓ Complete
R3 F1 Circumvent working capital bottleneckWhat is the CLO II? How is working capital freed? PP-C: Calmly purchases civil claims at 40% of face value, pursues as principal. Removes financing obligation from pool. Casehub v Wolf Cola [2017] — commercial interest test passed. ✓ Complete
R4 F2 Circumvent customer doubt about valueWhat is the Want Block? How is it resolved? Want Block: will the pool survive long enough for EV to materialise? Pool insurance resolves this. Framing theory (Kahneman & Tversky): loss frame at point of final refusal. ✓ Complete
R5 F2 Eliminate biggest learning disruptionWhat is the Use Block? How is it reduced? Use Block: product requires trust in an unknown operator for a high-stakes decision. ITP-mediated adoption: ITP's existing authority carries the referral. Rogers (2003) — trusted intermediary as adoption mechanism. ✓ Complete
R6 F2 Circumvent cash flow constraintWhat is the Buy Block? How is cash flow managed? Defendant pays deferred settlement (14–21 days post-notification). Claimant contribution timed after outcome certainty. Amortisation via outcome-contingent structure. ✓ Complete
R7 F2 Activate gateway partnersWho are the gateway partners? How are they activated? ITPs (housing associations, HR departments, online marketplaces, payment providers). Unifying mission: build a bigger, better ecosystem. Guiding policy: replacement, not addition. Crux: defendant reach through existing ITP authority. SR5 in progress. → In progress
R8 F3 Create a switching costWhat accretes value over time? What is lost on switching? EV calibration data: ITP-specific outcomes dataset is non-portable and compounds with tenure and claim volume. Switching destroys the accuracy edge that justifies the EV methodology. ✓ Complete
R9 F3 Circumvent supplier/partner leverageWhat is the scarce resource? How is it controlled? Independent assessment body (university law clinic or equivalent). Scarcity manufactured through accreditation + proprietary methodology licence. Portfolio diversification (≤30% concentration per ITP). ✓ Complete
R10 F3 Establish a competitive moatWhat makes the position structurally durable? Data moat: claim outcomes dataset grows with volume, enabling EV accuracy competitors cannot replicate without equivalent history. Network effect: more ITPs → more claim types → better EV calibration. ✓ Complete

The model stack

Six models constitute the full design definition. Each model has a defined input set, produces specific outputs, and connects to at least one other model in the stack. The CDM (Canonical Data Model) governs all inputs and confidence tiers — the financial simulation reads from it rather than maintaining independent assumptions.
