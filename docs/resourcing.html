Section 5

AOM gaps — activities implied but not fully specified

These are activities implied by the design but either absent from the AOM or deliberately flagged as deferred. They do not add cost lines to the current model — they are flagged for later architecture decisions.

Gap 1 — Legal counsel (not in AOM)

The AOM specifies FCA/SRA exchange-level authorisation as a finance and compliance function, but does not name a Legal Director or legal counsel role. A platform operating under FCA authorisation, managing SRA-interface compliance (the without-prejudice framework, assessment org contracts, pool operator agreements), and growing into securitisation architecture will require either a senior in-house legal hire or a substantial external legal retainer from Stage 2. The AOM names "Legal Director" in the Section 2 HQ card (in the fin-sim feed, Group 5: "CEO, COO, Legal Director, Compliance, Finance") but does not specify them in Section 6 or Section 7. This role is absent from the Section 6 AOM matrix — it appears to have been anticipated in the cost description but not structured as a role. At Stage 2+, a dedicated Legal Director at £100–£120k is likely required alongside the Compliance manager. This has not been added to the resourcing model — it should be flagged as a cost the fin-sim may be undercounting by £125–£150k/yr (fully-loaded) from Stage 2.

Gap 2 — HR and people operations (not in AOM)

The AOM does not specify an HR or people operations function. At Stage 1 (5.5 FTE), HR is manageable by the CEO. From Stage 3 (13.5 FTE HQ + territory managers + LMU platform staff), structured HR — employment contracts, performance processes, recruiter relationships — warrants at least a part-time HR specialist. This is an omission in the AOM, not a modelling choice. Estimated cost: £40–50k/yr (0.5 FTE) from Stage 3 onward. Not included in the current model but flagged as an AOM gap.

Gap 3 — Securitisation infrastructure (explicitly deferred)

AOM Section 6 marks "securitisation vehicle management, SPV operations, investor relations infrastructure at institutional scale, AML compliance for investor onboarding" as [REQUIRES later architecture]. These are not omissions — they are explicitly deferred. They will add HQ cost at Stage 3/at-scale when the securitisation bridge capital is raised. The AOM and this resourcing model correctly exclude them. They will need to be sized when securitisation architecture is developed.

Gap 4 — Marketing and brand (not in AOM)

The AOM explicitly states "None at LMU level. Marketing sits at HQ and ITP." — but then does not specify a marketing function at HQ. The Section 6 HQ matrix Marketing column references "ITP outcomes reports (primary C8 lock-in mechanism)" and "co-branded member communications" as commercial team activities. There is no named marketing hire. At exchange scale, brand positioning and institutional communications (for ITP partner acquisition and for investor relations) may warrant a part-time or contractor marketing resource from Stage 2. Not currently in the model — AOM gap, not a design choice.

Section 6

Tier labels and confidence note

Every assumption in this model is labelled. The distinction matters: Tier 2 derivations follow logically from AOM content and can be updated when the AOM is updated. Tier 3 rates require external validation but are market-observable. Tier 4 inputs are guesses that should be replaced by empirical data at the earliest opportunity.

Tier Meaning Items in this model
T2 Derived from AOM. The number follows logically from what the AOM states. If the AOM changes, the number changes — there is no independent empirical input required. ITP account manager headcount (derived from AOM C8 reporting workload formula). Insurance curation 0.5 FTE (AOM C4 directly states ~0.5 FTE). EV methodology analyst 0.75 FTE (AOM C9 + C10 sum). HQ cost per claim at each stage (mathematical output of derived headcount ÷ claim volume).
T3 Market benchmark. Salary rates use 2025 UK tech/fintech/legaltech benchmarks, sourced from published salary surveys (Glassdoor, Totaljobs, Reed, Robert Half 2025). Verifiable by checking comparable postings. Not guaranteed — actual salaries will depend on hiring market conditions and candidates. All salary rates in this model. On-cost rate of 25% (NI 13.8% + pension 5% + benefits ~6.2%).
T4 Unvalidated assumption. Cannot be derived from the AOM and cannot be directly observed in market data. Requires empirical validation — typically from a pilot or from direct conversations with candidates or operators. Module claim capacity of 480/yr (40/month × 12) — this is inherited from the fin-sim and is itself a T4 assumption. If module capacity differs, every HQ cost/claim figure changes. The at-scale module count of 38 — also inherited from fin-sim T4. Neither is introduced by this resourcing model; both are pre-existing T4 inputs in the fin-sim that this model inherits.
Net effect on fin-sim FMOS. The derived HQ cost figures are lower than the T4 estimates at Stages 1–3 and higher at scale. The largest change is at-scale: £1,135k derived vs £900k assumed — a difference of £235k/yr, or ~£13/claim at 18,240 claims. If the fin-sim at-scale cost floor was £245/claim, the revised figure after correcting the HQ line would be approximately £258/claim. At the price ceiling of £650/claim: FMOS = (650 − 258) ÷ 650 = 60.3% — still above the 25% IVE gate and still above 60%, but narrowed from 62.3%. The at-scale model remains viable. The Legal Director gap (Gap 1) adds a further ~£8/claim from Stage 2 onward if that hire is made — flagged but not included here.
