Screen 1 — Referral form

  • 1
    URL token encodes ITP identity — no login required. Reduces friction for the referring practitioner.
  • 2
    Three sections keep cognitive load low. Each section is one job: who is the client, what happened, have they consented.
  • 3
    Harm type + company + timing are structured inputs (dropdowns/radios) not free text — keeps completion time ≤5 min and feeds WP1 eligibility criteria directly.
  • 4
    Consent checkbox is legally required before Calmly can contact the claimant. Hard gate — submit disabled without it.
  • 5
    NTP fee reminder in footer reinforces value to the ITP without making it the lead message.

Screen 2 — Submission confirmation

  • 1
    Confirmation anchors the ITP's mental model — they know exactly what happens next. Builds trust in Calmly's process.
  • 2
    Referral reference number shown prominently — the ITP can quote this if a client asks for an update.
  • 3
    "Refer another client" is the primary CTA — reinforces the SOP behaviour we're trying to establish.
  • 4
    "Referral history" greyed out at pilot stage — placeholder for the OP5 reporting dashboard (built later, once Part P1 T4 test passes).

Screen 3 — Outcomes notification

  • 1
    Outcome notification closes the loop for the ITP — they know what happened to their client. This is the OP5 trigger: individual notification now, aggregate quarterly report later.
  • 2
    "52 days" and settlement confirmation are the proof points the ITP needs to keep referring. Resolution speed and success rate are what they'll cite when recommending Calmly to colleagues.
  • 3
    The tone is personal, not transactional. ITPs are relationship-oriented — they referred a real person they know.
  • 4
    Quarterly report preview sets the expectation for OP5 aggregate reporting without requiring it to be built at pilot stage.

Screen 1 — Referral queue

  • 1
    Queue view is the intake specialist's primary workspace. Simple table — no dashboard complexity needed at pilot stage.
  • 2
    NEW badge auto-clears when the specialist opens the referral. Helps with triage across multiple incoming referrals.
  • 3
    FLAGGED row (Patricia Williams) shows the limitation flag surfaced automatically when harm_timing = '6+ years'. Specialist reviews this before contacting the claimant — saves a wasted call.
  • 4
    Referral source (ITP name) visible in the list — helps the specialist understand the claimant's context before opening.
  • 5
    [Open] is the only action from this view. Keeps the interface clean — no bulk actions, no status changes from the list.

Screen 2 — Referral detail

  • 1
    All referral data shown in one view — specialist doesn't need to navigate to understand the case before calling the claimant.
  • 2
    Limitation check is auto-computed from harm_timing field. PASS in green, REVIEW in amber (6+ years), FAIL in red. Removes manual date calculation from the specialist's workflow.
  • 3
    NTP fee attribution visible here — specialist can confirm the right ITP is credited before opening the intake. Catches any token errors before they affect the fee log.
  • 4
    Three action states: Begin (most cases), Flag (needs a second opinion), Reject (ineligible on its face). Keeps the decision simple.
  • 5
    Activity log grows as the case progresses — audit trail without a separate system.

Screen 3 — Eligibility checklist (WP1)

  • 1
    2 of 6 criteria are auto-filled from the referral form data — reduces specialist effort and errors. Items 1, 2, 5 are pre-computed.
  • 2
    Items 3, 4, 6 require specialist judgement — confirm from conversation with the claimant or a quick check.
  • 3
    Outcome is auto-calculated: all 6 pass = ELIGIBLE, any fail = INELIGIBLE (failing criterion highlighted red). No manual verdict — removes discretion drift.
  • 4
    "Save and return later" for cases where the specialist needs to call the claimant before completing the checklist.
  • 5
    This screen IS the WP1 minimum build — a structured checklist, not a separate system. The simplest thing that could work.
