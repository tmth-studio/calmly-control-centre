Section 2

ITP function and the two-sided test

The ITP is not a distribution channel. A distribution channel passes volume — it cannot change what a defendant believes about their liability. The ITP is a gateway partner: an institution with a pre-existing relationship with both the claimant (as client) and, critically, with the defendant (as regulatory peer, trade counterparty, or institutional correspondent).

That second relationship is the structural advantage. When a defendant bank receives a Calmly notification forwarded through a firm it already has an account relationship or institutional standing with, it is not reading a demand from an unknown fintech. It is receiving a structured prompt from a counterpart whose credibility is already priced in. The defendant's conviction about liability shifts before any legal process begins.

This two-sided function must both work. Claimant origination without defendant notification advantage means Calmly competes on volume only — structurally identical to a CMC aggregator and indistinguishable from conventional BFF. Defendant notification without claimant origination means the ITP is forwarding notifications for claims sourced elsewhere, which neither creates supply nor tests the ITP's client-facing workflow. Both sides must pass in the same pilot.

The test is operational, not aspirational. The CDR gate requires demonstrated willingness at the staff level — not executive buy-in. An ITP whose compliance team approves the template but whose frontline staff find the referral form too complex has not passed. The pilot is designed to surface that gap before the portal is built.

The notification template must be structured as an informational document only. It must not constitute commencement of proceedings, legal advice to the defendant, or regulated legal services under the Legal Services Act 2007. The ITP forwards it through their own institutional channel — the defendant's response to institutional vs. fintech-direct demand is the mechanism by which Calmly's settlement rate advantage over conventional BFF is produced.

Section 3

Five ITP type profiles

Five types are assessed for the due diligence pilot. The selection covers the range of regulatory frameworks, client access points, and commercial structures that Calmly will encounter at scale. No single type is assumed to dominate — concentration limits (≤30% from any single type) require at minimum three types to pass the pilot.

1. Claims management companies (CMCs)

Regulated by: Financial Conduct Authority · FSMA 2000 · CMC authorisation
Pilot priority: High Volume: High
CMCs are the most structurally aligned ITP type. Financial mis-selling origination is their core business — they already run intake workflows, assess claim validity, and manage claimant expectations. Many hold historical dispute databases from PPI and packaged account cycles. Embedding Calmly as a resolution channel supplements their existing operation rather than displacing it: they retain the claimant relationship while Calmly performs the matching and settlement function.
Claimant origination Existing intake infrastructure. Staff trained in financial mis-selling assessment. Referral form completion expected to be fastest of all five types — prior experience with structured intake processes.
Defendant notification CMCs have existing institutional relationships with defendant banks through prior settlement activity. Forwarding a structured notification template is operationally routine. High forwarding rate expected.
CMC must not conduct "claims management activity" (FSMA s.419A) beyond what its FCA authorisation covers — Calmly's FCA authorisation holds for the exchange function; the split of activity must be documented to avoid regulatory overlap.
Outcomes-linked referral fee (FCA-permissible). Fee triggered on successful WP3 match and accepted settlement, not on claim submission. Flat per-referral fee acceptable as secondary structure for volume commitments.
Highest expected pass rate on both sides. Sets the operational benchmark against which other types are compared. First to recruit for pilot cohort.

2. Solicitors and law firms

Regulated by: Solicitors Regulation Authority · SRA Code of Conduct 2019
Pilot priority: High Volume: Medium–High
High-street general practice and specialist financial litigation firms encounter historical mis-selling claims during client reviews and estate matters. Clients rarely surface these claims proactively — they emerge during conveyancing, probate, or debt restructuring when documentation is reviewed. The solicitor is the trusted professional who surfaces the claim; embedding a Calmly referral path at that moment converts a passive discovery into an active submission. Specialist financial litigation firms are particularly valuable: they already assess claim merit and hold institutional credibility with defendant banks.
Claimant origination Natural — the solicitor's professional duty already includes identifying claims the client may not have raised. Referral form completion requires brief training on which claim types qualify, but the workflow integration is low-friction.
Defendant notification Structurally strong — solicitors hold institutional credibility with defendant banks. However, forwarding a Calmly-drafted template requires the solicitor to confirm it does not constitute regulated legal services on their behalf. Legal opinion required before this is tested.
SRA Code 2019, r.5.1: referral to a non-SRA firm for a fee requires disclosure to the client and client consent. LASPO referral fee ban does not apply to financial services claims (confirmed — but legal opinion required for FCA file). Template forwarding must not constitute regulated legal services.
Outcomes-linked referral fee with SRA-compliant disclosure mechanism built into referral form. Client consent captured at point of referral. Fixed per-referral alternative available for high-volume firms.
SRA compliance review is on the critical path before solicitor types can be engaged. Pilot should be sequenced after legal opinion is received (10 working days). High priority because solicitor-forwarded notifications carry the strongest defendant conviction shift — institutional credibility is highest of all five types.

3. Independent financial advisers (IFAs)

Regulated by: Financial Conduct Authority · FCA Handbook · IFA authorisation
Pilot priority: Medium Volume: Low–Medium
IFAs conduct regular client reviews and suitability assessments. These reviews routinely surface prior-adviser recommendations that would not pass current suitability standards — packaged account mis-selling, unsuitable investment advice, or products sold without adequate disclosure. The IFA is in a position of trust and has the full picture of the client's financial history. This makes claim identification accurate and claim quality high. Volume per IFA firm is low, but false-positive rate is minimal.
Claimant origination High quality, lower volume. IFAs identify genuine claims with high precision. Referral form completion is well within capability — IFAs already produce structured client documentation.
Defendant notification Moderate institutional credibility with defendant banks — less than solicitors, comparable to CMCs. Forwarding a notification template is operationally feasible but may require internal compliance sign-off per firm.
IFAs must not conduct claims management activity without FCA CMC authorisation (FSMA s.419A). The referral must be clearly structured as client signposting, not claims assessment or management on Calmly's behalf.
Outcomes-linked referral fee. Volume is low per firm, so fee level must be meaningful per accepted claim. Aggregation across IFA networks (e.g. via network head) is the preferred scale mechanism.
Medium priority — valuable for claim quality, not volume. Best piloted after CMC type establishes the operational baseline. IFA networks (St. James's Place, Openwork, Quilter) should be approached as aggregate pilots rather than individual firms.

4. Debt management and IVA practitioners

Regulated by: FCA (debt management) · Insolvency Practitioners Association (IVA)
Pilot priority: Medium Volume: High
Debt management and IVA practitioners work daily with financially distressed households — the same population most likely to have embedded mis-selling claims from the packaged account and PPI era. These practitioners hold full financial history for their clients and have a professional incentive to reduce the debt burden: a successful Calmly settlement reduces outstanding liability and improves the IVA or debt plan outcome. The complementarity with C5 (claim management removes the litigation burden from a distressed household) is structurally clean. This is a natural extension of an existing client service, not a new activity.
Claimant origination Very strong. Practitioners have full client financial history and can identify qualifying claims as part of standard onboarding. Volume per firm is highest of all five types — distressed client bases are dense with historical mis-selling exposure.
Defendant notification Weaker institutional credibility with defendant banks in this capacity — debt management firms are creditors' agents in IVA context, which may create adversarial framing. Template forwarding feasible but defendant conviction effect may be lower than solicitor or IFA types.
FCA debt management authorisation does not cover claims management activity — the referral must be clearly outside FSMA s.419A scope. IPA-regulated IVA practitioners face additional considerations around client consent where the estate is already subject to insolvency proceedings.
Outcomes-linked referral fee. Fee structure must account for the possibility that a successful claim offsets creditor recovery — needs legal review to confirm no conflict with IVA supervisor duties. Volume-tiered fee schedule preferred.
High volume potential justifies medium priority despite defendant notification concern. Pilot should assess whether defendant conviction effect is sufficient — if not, this type is claimant-origination only, with defendant notification routed through a different ITP type for the same claim.

5. Consumer organisations and trade bodies

Not referral-fee regulated in same manner · varies by organisation type
Pilot priority: Medium Volume: Low
Which?, trade unions, credit unions, and professional associations hold high public trust and are already positioned as consumer advocates. Their involvement creates a legitimacy signal that neither CMCs nor solicitors can replicate — a Calmly referral from Which? carries consumer endorsement, not legal or commercial pressure. For the defendant notification side, institutional credibility with banks is strong: major consumer organisations have established dialogue channels with defendant institutions through FCA mediation and parliamentary processes. These are strategic anchor clients — low volume but outsized legitimacy effect.
Claimant origination Moderate. Consumer orgs receive and log member complaints but lack structured intake workflows. Referral form must be very simple — the staff completing it are general caseworkers, not claims specialists. Completion time pilot most critical for this type.
Defendant notification Strong. Consumer orgs already communicate with defendant institutions through established channels. A Calmly notification forwarded through Which? or a trade union carries institutional weight that fintech-direct demand cannot match. Forwarding rate expected to be high once template is approved internally.
Consumer organisations are not typically referral-fee regulated in the same way as SRA or FCA firms, but must confirm their charitable or membership constitution permits fee-based referral activity. Which? as a commercial trading arm operates differently from a trade union — each organisation type requires a separate constitutional review.
Donation or partnership model preferred over direct referral fee for charitable/membership organisations. Commercial trading arms (e.g. Which? Ltd) can accept outcomes-linked fees. Trade unions: member benefit framing, fee-sharing with central fund.
Strategic anchor clients — C7 FAIL fallback architecture depends on consumer org direct model. Even if solicitor and CMC types pass, at least one consumer org should be in the pilot to validate the legitimacy-signalling hypothesis and to de-risk C7 architecture against concentration risk.
Section 4

Due diligence protocol

Each ITP type goes through the same five-step protocol. The sequence is deliberate: commercial assessment gates operational assessment; operational assessment gates the pilot. No ITP type proceeds to referral form pilot without clearing compliance assessment first.

  1. Commercial assessment Establish referral fee structure, volume commitment, and exclusivity position. Confirm that the ITP type's regulatory framework permits the proposed commercial relationship. For FCA-authorised types, confirm the outcomes-linked fee does not constitute regulated activity on the ITP's part. For SRA-regulated types, confirm referral fee disclosure mechanism is built into the workflow. Document volume commitment (minimum referrals per quarter) and exclusivity terms — Calmly's concentration limit policy (≤30% from any single type) means no exclusivity at type level, but firm-level exclusivity is permissible.
  2. Operational assessment Assess staff capacity available for referral activity, systems access requirements (does the referral portal need to integrate with an existing case management system?), and workflow integration points. Identify who within the firm will complete referral forms — frontline caseworkers vs. specialist staff changes the training requirement. Map the defendant notification forwarding chain: who approves the template, who sends it, through which channel, and what internal compliance sign-off is required before forwarding.
  3. Compliance assessment Review regulatory status, referral fee rules specific to ITP type, and confirm whether SRA or FCA legal opinion is required before engagement. For solicitor types, the compliance assessment is blocked on SRA legal opinion (Section 6 below). For other types, confirm FSMA s.419A scope is not breached. Confirm the defendant notification template does not constitute commencement of proceedings, legal advice, or regulated legal services under the Legal Services Act 2007 for the forwarding ITP.
  4. Referral form pilot Test with 5 untrained staff members per ITP type. Measure: completion time (pass: ≤5 minutes), error rate (pass: zero critical errors), and drop-out rate (pass: 100% completion). Critical error is defined as any field entry that would cause incorrect claim routing or create an invalid claim record. Non-critical errors (formatting, optional field omissions) are logged but do not count against the pass criteria. All 5 participants must pass. A single failure triggers a form redesign iteration before the type is re-tested.
  5. Defendant notification pilot Test forwarding of the notification template in a structured pilot. Measure: forwarding rate (pass: ≥90% of staff forward without modification), legal review time (pass: template reviewable without ITP's own legal counsel — target under 2 hours for an individual ITP staff member), and modifications requested (pass: zero substantive modifications — cosmetic letterhead additions are permitted). If modifications are requested, document the specific change and assess whether it is acceptable within the template structure. A pattern of similar modification requests across ITP types informs a template revision.

Fail protocol

A single ITP type failure does not constitute a C7 fail. The type is excluded from the scale architecture — Calmly's concentration limit framework absorbs the loss provided ≥3 types pass.

C7 overall PASS condition: ≥3 ITP types pass both referral form and defendant notification sides. The concentration constraint (≤30% per type) is satisfiable with 3 types at roughly equal volume contribution. A 4- or 5-type pass gives additional resilience.
⚠ C7 FAIL condition: fewer than 3 ITP types pass either side, or the two-sided function is demonstrably impossible across all five types (e.g. defendant notification forwarding rate is below 50% across all types). FAIL triggers a gateway architecture redesign — replace ITP model with direct-to-claimant origination via consumer org anchor and defendant notification via Calmly's own institutional channel. This is the fallback, not the preference: it removes the defendant conviction mechanism that drives the settlement rate advantage.

If a single side fails for a given type (referral form passes, notification fails), that type is recorded as claimant-origination only. It contributes to supply volume but not to defendant notification — the notification function for claims it originates is routed through a passing type where an institutional relationship exists with the same defendant bank.

Section 6

SRA compliance review protocol

The solicitor ITP type is on the critical path for two reasons: it carries the highest defendant conviction effect of any type, and it requires a documented legal opinion before any firm can be engaged. The opinion cannot be substituted by internal Calmly legal review — it must come from an independent firm instructed specifically on the questions below.

Scope of the legal opinion

  1. SRA Code 2019, r.5.1 compliance Does Calmly's referral fee structure breach r.5.1 of the SRA Code of Conduct 2019 when the ITP is a solicitor or law firm? Specifically: does the outcomes-linked fee structure satisfy the disclosure requirement to the client, and what form must that disclosure take to avoid a conduct breach? What consent mechanism is required at point of referral form completion?
  2. Defendant notification template characterisation Does forwarding Calmly's defendant notification template, in its current form, constitute (a) commencement of proceedings under CPR Part 7 or a pre-action protocol, (b) legal advice to the defendant under the Legal Services Act 2007, or (c) regulated legal services requiring the forwarding solicitor to take responsibility for the content? The opinion must address each limb separately. If any limb is positive, a redrafted template must be produced and reviewed.
  3. LASPO referral fee ban — scope confirmation Confirm on the record that the LASPO 2012 Part 2 ban on referral fees in personal injury litigation does not extend to financial services mis-selling claims. This is understood to be the position, but a documented legal opinion is required for Calmly's FCA regulatory file. The opinion should cite the relevant statutory provision and any Law Society guidance that confirms the position for financial services claims specifically.
  4. Agency liability — defendant notification forwarding Does the solicitor ITP's act of forwarding the notification template through its institutional channel create any agency liability toward the defendant? Specifically: does the defendant acquire a legal relationship with the forwarding solicitor by virtue of having received the notification through that channel? And does the solicitor ITP incur any professional duty to the defendant by acting as the notification conduit?
Timeline: legal opinion commissioned within 5 working days of CDR gate approval. Opinion delivered within 10 working days. Solicitor ITP pilot cohort assembled in parallel but held pending opinion. If opinion identifies a template defect, a revised template is produced within 5 working days and reviewed internally before solicitor pilot commences.
⚠ Sequencing constraint. No solicitor or law firm ITP may be engaged — commercially or operationally — until the legal opinion is received and reviewed. Any engagement before that date creates an undocumented compliance exposure in Calmly's FCA authorisation file.
