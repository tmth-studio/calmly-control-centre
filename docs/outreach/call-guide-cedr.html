Context to give

What Calmly is:

  • AI-first consumer dispute resolution platform, not a law firm, not a claims management company
  • Consumers with similar disputes pool their positions — a housing deposit dispute, a marketplace scam — into a claimant pool
  • The platform produces an EV (Expected Value) report: the expected settlement value of a claim, given case strength, comparable precedent, and pool size
  • The EV report is served simultaneously to claimant and defendant — it is the price discovery mechanism that moves both parties to a rational settlement without going to court
  • Calmly earns 20% of gross settlement proceeds — no upfront fee to anyone
  • Architecture is complete. The CDR gate (a rigorous systems engineering verification) has been passed

Why CEDR specifically:

  • The EV report needs to be independently endorsed to carry weight with defendants
  • CEDR already runs Expert Determination services and the IDRS brand — per-case expert sign-off is exactly the structure needed
  • CEDR has publicly positioned itself as an innovator in ADR access; Calmly's target range (£200–£25,000, housing deposits and marketplace disputes) is the gap CEDR has said they want to serve

"What I'm looking for is a conversation with your Expert Determination team — 30 minutes — to walk through the CDR architecture and understand whether the endorsement model makes sense for CEDR. I'm not asking for a commitment. I want to know if there's a fit."

Objections and responses

"We'd need to review the methodology before committing."
"Absolutely — that's exactly what I'm proposing. A 30-minute session with your Expert Determination team to walk through the CDR architecture. No commitment expected from that conversation."
"We're not sure this fits our current service lines."
"Your IDRS brand already runs adjudication schemes for specific sectors. What we're proposing is structurally similar — a per-case expert sign-off using your existing panel. The difference is that the case arrives pre-assessed, so your panel is validating a methodology output rather than running the assessment from scratch."
"What's the volume at launch?"
"We're starting with 25 to 50 cases in the first cohort — deliberately small, so both sides can assess the model. We expect to scale to 40 cases per month per ITP relationship, with three ITPs operational within six months. At that run rate, CEDR would be endorsing roughly 700 cases a year."
"Who else are you speaking to about endorsement?"
"CEDR is the first call. Your Expert Determination infrastructure is the closest match to what we need, and your positioning on ADR access fits the claim types we're targeting."
"We'd need to charge a fee — how does that work?"
"We'd expect CEDR to charge a per-report fee. The simplest structure is that the fee comes from the claimant's recovery proceeds — same principle as Calmly's own fee. We can discuss fixed-fee per case as an alternative. This is a commercial arrangement, not a charity partnership."
"Is this regulated?"
"Calmly is not a claims management company — we're a technology platform producing an assessment. The endorsement role you'd play is expert opinion on a methodology output, which is what your Expert Determination panel does already. We have legal scope documentation I can share before any meeting."
