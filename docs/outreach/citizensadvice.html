Subject
The gap between advice and recovery

Peter,

Citizens Advice advisers see the same patterns repeatedly — landlord deposit withheld, wages unpaid, marketplace fraud — and the honest answer they give is: here's how to navigate this, but you're on your own when it comes to actually getting the money back.

Court is slow and expensive. Ombudsmen cover a narrow set of cases. Solicitors are inaccessible for small amounts. For most people, the advice ends without a pathway that produces a monetary outcome.

I'm building a recovery mechanism that complements exactly what Citizens Advice does. Consumers with similar disputes pool into a collective position — which makes recovery economically viable for claims that are too small to pursue individually. There's no upfront cost; we earn a fee only when money is recovered.

The model I want to discuss with you is one where your advisers, when they identify a recoverable dispute, have somewhere concrete to send people — not another signposting dead-end, but a mechanism that actually moves money.

I'm an early-stage founder and this is a genuine conversation, not a pitch. Would you have 20 minutes in the next couple of weeks?
