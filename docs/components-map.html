Component / Activity / Owner / Journey Map

A cross-referenced view of the nine product flows that constitute Calmly at scale. For each flow: the activities required to produce and deliver it, the requirements drawn from the modelling, the HR positions accountable, and the customer or partner journeys in which it appears. Components are the products; activities are what HR roles do to make and manage each flow.

What this document is

Reading the AOM, ARM and Fin Sim together exposes a structure that no single document carries: which HR positions touch which journeys, where dual-production chains create supervision dependencies, and where the LASPO regulatory floor sits. The AOM gives the at-scale flows between products. The ARM gives the personnel who make and manage them. The Fin Sim gives the unit economics that constrain volume and pricing. The CTM gives the state changes each product drives. This page is the synthesis — one row per component, with everything cross-referenced.

How to read the detail cards

Each component below carries the activities required to produce or deliver it (drawn from ARM Section 2), the requirements derived from the modelling (categorised by volume, regulatory, financial, structural, quality), the HR positions accountable (direct producer, managerial oversight, support roles), and the journey actors who experience the product. Component detail — flows, activities, requirements, owners

CP1 Marketing & lead generation Communications product
State change: Unaware SME with active dispute → aware of Calmly as a viable route. Recipient: Claimant (SME).
Activities required
  • Create and manage paid search / social campaigns targeting SMEs with active disputes
  • Produce and publish content (guides, landing pages, case study formats)
  • Monitor and optimise lead acquisition cost per qualified inquiry
  • Qualify inbound inquiries against eligibility criteria (claim value, dispute type, jurisdiction)
  • Hand qualified leads to Litigation Associate for WP1 assessment
Requirements derived from modelling
Volume

Must generate ~36,480 qualified inquiries/year at full scale to support 18,240 purchased claims (×2 driver from 50% conversion rate).

Cost

Blended CAC £22.20/claim at scale: 60% ITP-referred at £25 NTP fee, 40% organic at £18 (Fin Sim C7).

Eligibility filter

Claim value, dispute type, jurisdiction must align with PCO (commercial fast track, £10k–£50k UK SME disputes).

Structural — C7

Blended channel strategy: ITP gateway provides credibility and lower CAC; organic provides volume floor not dependent on ITP relationships.

WP1 Case analysis & valuation report Working product
State change: Interested claimant → committed claimant. Knows risk-weighted value of selling the claim, trusts the assessment enough to sign WP2. Recipient: Claimant (SME).
Activities required
  • Gather claim details from claimant (intake interview, document collection)
  • Categorise legal standing of the claim
  • Quantify dispute value from documentary evidence
  • Gather and weight supporting evidence
  • Cross-check defendant position (financial capacity, prior conduct, enforcement risk)
  • Assess probability of verdict (RCF base-rate methodology)
  • Assess probability of enforcement
  • Share analysis with claimant and agree purchase price
Requirements derived from modelling
Volume — ×2 driver

50% purchase conversion means 2 WP1 assessments per purchased claim. At scale: ~36,480 assessments/year vs 18,240 purchases. Staffing must size to inquiry volume not purchase volume (ARM Constraint 3).

Quality

Risk-weighted valuation must be defensible — it becomes the basis for the WP2 purchase price the claimant signs against.

Trust

Claimant must trust the assessment enough to assign the claim. Defensibility of method matters as much as the number.

Throughput

Process per Litigation Associate FTE: total activity hours at scale ÷ hours/FTE/year. Bottleneck for case pipeline.

WP2 Claim purchase contract Working product
State change: Committed claimant → contracted claimant. Legally transfers the claim to Calmly. Recipient: Claimant (SME).
Activities required
  • Generate claim purchase contract (templated, parameterised from WP1)
  • Share with claimant for signature
  • File executed contract
Requirements derived from modelling
Legal — SRA

Contract must be a valid, SRA-compliant claim assignment. The legal transfer is what makes Calmly the party-in-interest for WP3–WP6.

Trigger sequence

Only generated after WP1 purchase price is agreed. Premature generation breaks the trust chain.

Audit

Executed contract must be retrievable as an auditable record for compliance and Pool Operator portfolio assurance.

Speed

Time from WP1 agreement to WP2 execution is a working-capital input: longer = larger WC float on assessment org payment lag.

WP3 C3 EV assessment report Working product · Dual-role production
State change: Overconfident / motivated-reasoning defendant → aware defendant. An independent, financially-committed party has assessed their probability of winning as lower than they believe. Recipient: Defendant. Special: The only component with a two-role production chain at the direct level.
Activities required
Draft — Law Student Assessor
  • Receive brief and case file from Litigation Associate
  • Research comparable cases using Reference Class Forecasting methodology
  • Analyse factual position of claimant and defendant
  • Apply RCF base-rate scoring to produce probability-of-verdict estimate
  • Draft EV assessment report to Calmly template (defendant-facing)
  • Submit draft to Qualified Supervisor for review and sign-off
Sign-off — Qualified Supervisor
  • Review student draft for factual accuracy and methodology compliance
  • Correct errors or return for revision with specific notes
  • Apply professional sign-off (LASPO/SRA requirement)
  • Dispatch finalised WP3 to Litigation Associate for inclusion in WP4 settlement pack
  • Monitor student quality across the cohort; flag patterns to Claims Manager
Requirements derived from modelling
Regulatory — LASPO

Qualified Supervisor sign-off required regardless of volume (ARM Constraint 1). Creates a cost floor that does not respond to volume — efficient only at 200+ cases/year.

Methodology

RCF base-rate scoring; explicit independence from claimant; defendant-facing template that establishes financial commitment of the assessor.

Volume seasonality

Student capacity batched to term-time (ARM Constraint 2). Either buffer WP3 production ahead of settlement timelines or partner with multiple universities to smooth supply.

Mechanism — C3

Disrupts defendant motivated reasoning. The report is structurally different from a settlement demand — it is an independent probability assessment from a party with money at stake.

Pipeline dependency

WP3 is the input that makes WP4 work. Without finalised, signed-off WP3, the WP4 settlement demand has no anchor to disrupt defendant anchoring.

WP4 Settlement offer + negotiation timeline Working product
State change: Notified defendant → defendant with explicit, escalating cost-of-delay. Settling now becomes financially rational. Recipient: Defendant.
Activities required
  • Prepare settlement demand letter incorporating C3 EV report (WP3) and negotiation timeline
  • Send to defendant (SRA-compliant delivery)
  • Log receipt and response
  • Advance timeline milestones — each milestone tied to escalating offer terms (links to WP6)
Requirements derived from modelling
Bundle dependency

Must incorporate signed-off WP3. Sequencing WP4 ahead of WP3 sign-off breaks the C3 mechanism.

Regulatory — SRA

Defendant notification must be SRA-compliant in form and content.

Mechanism — C2 timeline

Explicit, escalating cost-of-delay is the design innovation. Without it, WP4 is a static demand letter.

Tracking

Receipt logging and response capture feed Pool Operator portfolio data and WP6 escalation triggers.

WP5 Settlement proceeds distribution Working product
State change: Settled case → completed transaction. Claimant receives net proceeds; case is closed. Recipient: Claimant (SME).
Activities required
  • Receive settlement funds (from defendant via PP1)
  • Calculate net proceeds after Calmly platform fee
  • Pay claimant
  • Close case record (feeds outcomes data to PartP1)
Requirements derived from modelling
Financial

Platform fee at 25% FMOS target = £344/claim (revised cost floor £258 ÷ (1 − 0.25)). At average settlement of £3,000 this is 11.5% (Fin Sim).

Reconciliation

Settlement funds must be tracked through Pool Operator before distribution — protects portfolio assurance position.

Speed — WC

Cash cycle affects WC peak (~£2.2M at scale). Faster proceeds turnaround reduces capital tied up.

Closure data

Case record at closure populates outcomes data feeding PartP1 and securitisation-readiness pack (Fin Sim Gate 2→3).

WP6 Escalating settlement offer (C2) Working product
State change: Non-responding defendant → defendant with revised, higher-cost offer tied to timeline milestone. Each delay costs more. Recipient: Defendant.
Activities required
  • Prepare and send revised settlement offers at each timeline milestone
  • Record defendant response
  • Assess whether to continue negotiation or escalate to court proceedings
Requirements derived from modelling
Trigger

Activated for non-responding defendants after WP4. Tied to negotiation timeline milestones, not arbitrary cadence.

Escalation logic

Each revised offer tied to a specific milestone with higher cost. Withdrawal of escalation must follow a documented decision path.

Court escalation

Decision to escalate to court must be approved by Claims Manager. ~25% of cases reach court (Fin Sim assumption).

Cost

Court-stage external counsel costs sit under Head of Legal & Ops consumables — modelled separately in Fin Sim as court_external_cost_per_case. Not in direct labour model.

PP1 Claim purchase payment + settlement receipt Payment product
State change: Contracted claimant → paid claimant; settled defendant → closed transaction. Recipients: Claimant (purchase price), Defendant (settlement receipt).
Activities required
  • Process claim purchase payment to claimant (immediately after WP2 execution)
  • Receive settlement payment from defendant (after WP5 negotiation closes)
  • Reconcile flows through Pool Operator portfolio accounts
  • Manage payment processing relationships (banking, settlement rails)
Requirements derived from modelling
Pool Operator integration

1–3 regional Pool Operators at scale (AOM). All PP1 flows route through Pool Operator accounts — Calmly platform fee is deducted at this point.

WC investment cost

£16.28 × (8% CoC ÷ 12) × 4.65 months weighted avg = £0.50/claim at 8% CoC (Fin Sim). Peak WC at full scale: ~£2.2M outstanding.

Payment processing

Banking and payment processing fees sit as consumables under Operations & Finance.

Working capital sizing

WC requirement compounds at Stage 1 (litigation funder capital, 25–30% CoC) — informs the Stage 1 capital raise (~£475–700k per Fin Sim Model 2).

PartP1 Outcomes report (ITP data — C8 lock-in) Partner product
State change: ITP referral relationship → C8 lock-in. ITP dependency on outcomes data increases switching cost over time. Recipient: ITP (Insolvency Tribunal Practitioner).
Activities required
  • Aggregate case outcomes data across LMUs (settlement rate, recovery rate, time-to-resolution)
  • Produce periodic outcomes report formatted per ITP partner requirements
  • Deliver via platform integration (digital, scheduled cadence)
  • Maintain ITP partner relationships; expand to 3+ partnerships by Stage 2 gate
  • Govern data quality and audit trail (links to C10)
Requirements derived from modelling
Mechanism — C8 lock-in

The outcomes report creates ITP switching cost via data dependency (AOM v4). Once ITPs use Calmly outcomes data in their own workflow, switching to a competitor means losing the historical view.

Aggregation

Cross-LMU outcomes — requires data infrastructure that spans all 38 modules with consistent definitions.

Cadence

Periodic delivery (frequency TBD). High enough to be useful to ITPs, low enough to keep ops cost down.

Quality — Gate 2→3

Must be investor-grade — feeds the securitisation-readiness pack at the Stage 2 to Stage 3 gate. ITP concentration limits (no single ITP >30% of claim supply) protect the data narrative.

Cross-reference — which components each HR position touches

One row per HR position. The chips show every component the role is accountable for (direct or supervisory).

HR position Components touched
Marketing Manager Direct CP1 Sole direct producer. One per venture, not volume-dependent.
Litigation Associate Direct WP1 WP2 WP4 WP5 WP6 PP1 Owns the full claim-lifecycle production chain. Headcount scales with case volume × 2 (50% conversion).
Law Student Assessor Direct WP3 Drafts only. Scales with purchased claim volume; capacity batched to term-time.
Qualified Supervisor Direct WP3 Sign-off only. 1 supervisor per 10–15 active students. Minimum 0.5 FTE for LASPO compliance regardless of volume.
Claims Manager Managerial WP1 WP4 WP6 Supervises Litigation Associates. Approves court escalations (WP6). 1 per 8–10 associates.
Head of Legal & Operations Managerial WP1 WP2 WP3 WP4 WP6 PartP1 Owns LASPO/SRA compliance position, law school partnerships, court strategy, data governance. One per venture; scales to geography/segment at Phase II.
Operations & Finance Support CP1 WP2 WP5 PP1 PartP1 Cross-cutting support. CRM, filing, payment execution, data aggregation. 1 FTE up to ~500 cases/year; +0.5 FTE per 300 thereafter.
Founder / CEO Venture-wide CP1 PartP1 External positioning, regulatory engagement, ITP and law school partnerships. Cost excluded from early-stage unit economics.
What this view exposes

The Litigation Associate is the single highest-touch direct role — six of nine components depend on it. The Head of Legal & Operations is the highest-touch oversight role and the only one that bridges all four product types. WP3 is the only dual-role direct production chain in the system. Operations & Finance is the connective tissue across communications, working and payment products.

Cross-reference — which components each journey actor experiences

One row per actor. The chips show every component that appears in that actor's journey, in approximate sequence.

Journey actor Components in their journey (in sequence)
Claimant (SME) Customer CP1 → WP1 → WP2 → PP1 → WP5 Full conversion: aware → committed → contracted → paid (purchase price) → paid (net proceeds). Five touchpoints across the lifecycle.
Defendant Customer WP3 → WP4 → WP6 → PP1 Awareness disruption → motivation → escalation (if non-responding) → settlement payment. Court only for the 25% that escalate.
ITP Partner CP1 → PartP1 Inbound: refers claimants into CP1. Outbound: receives PartP1 outcomes report. The C8 lock-in is built in the partner-product loop, not the customer-facing flow.
Pool Operator Partner WP5 PP1 All settlement money flows through Pool Operator portfolio accounts. Not customer-facing; sits between Calmly platform and Claimant/Defendant.
Assessment Org Partner WP1 Per AOM v4: centralised EV scoring body. Canonical name: "Assessment Org" (resolved 24 May 2026 — replaces earlier "Independent Assessment Organisation" label). Assessment Org produces WP1 (EV assessment). Law Student Assessor / Qualified Supervisor operates within the Assessment Org to produce WP1 outputs. WP3 (pool data aggregation) is a Pool Operator infrastructure responsibility, not an Assessment Org output.
Law school Partner WP3 Supplies the Law Student Assessor pipeline. Not a direct recipient. Multi-university partnerships smooth term-time seasonality (ARM Constraint 2).
Court External WP6 Escalation only — ~25% of cases. Triggers external counsel costs under Head of Legal & Ops. Modelled as court_external_cost_per_case in Fin Sim.
What this view exposes

The Claimant journey is the longest (five components) and spans communications, working and payment products. The Defendant journey is structurally shorter but carries the C2 negotiation-timeline and C3 EV-report mechanisms — the two design innovations that make settlement rational. The ITP appears in only two components but those two carry the C8 lock-in, the strongest competitive moat in the architecture. Pool Operator and Assessment Org are partner roles that sit in the flow but never customer-facing.
