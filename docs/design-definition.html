2. TP-4 open item — blocking CDR start

C7 (gateway partner activation — ITP referral protocol) is the only remaining Architecture Definition item. Until it is closed, the Part1 component specification cannot be finalised, and the build sequence cannot be validated. Part1 (ITP referral protocol) is build sequence item 1 — it is the critical path.

C7 activation pack — three deliverables outstanding

The following three sub-deliverables are open. All three are required before Part1 component specification can be finalised:

  • ITP due diligence pack — the package of material that enables an ITP (housing association, trade body, online marketplace, HR department) to evaluate and approve Calmly partnership within their governance process. Must address: SRA compliance, fee structure disclosure, data sharing framework, ITP staff burden.
  • Referral protocol — the operational document specifying exactly how an ITP staff member routes a qualifying claim to the Calmly exchange. Must be completable in ≤5 minutes per claim without legal training. Determines the Part1 interface design.
  • Outcomes reporting format — the quarterly summary and annual deep report template, designed from the ITP's institutional reporting needs backward. This is the C8 lock-in mechanism — the ITP embedding Calmly exchange data in its own stakeholder reporting. Must be agreed in principle before the C8 component specification can be finalised.

4. Build sequence — risk-ordered

Components are ordered by two criteria: dependency (what must exist before what) and risk (which components carry the highest assumption uncertainty). Risk takes precedence where dependency allows. T4 assumptions that FAIL at component validation are architectural signals — return to the BALM requirement responsible, not the execution plan.

1

Part1 — ITP referral protocol and portal T4 — highest risk

Rationale: risk-first. This is the C7 open item — the only remaining TP-4 closure task. Until Part1 is validated, the entire claimant supply chain and defendant notification mechanism is unproven. Every downstream component assumes ITP activation works.

T4 assumption being tested: ITPs will embed Calmly referral as a standard operational procedure and will forward defendant notifications through their institutional channel without modification.

If FAIL: the gateway partner architecture (C7) does not work. Return to C7 BALM design — redesign the activation mechanism before proceeding to any other component.

Blocked: must close C7 (ITP due diligence pack, referral protocol, outcomes reporting format) before this component spec can be finalised.

2

CP3 — defendant notification and liability buyout facilitation T4 — highest risk

Rationale: risk-first. The defendant settlement rate (70% target, minimum viable 60%) is the most consequential single assumption in the architecture — it drives FMOS, working capital, and the viability of the double-signal mechanism. This cannot be tested without live defendants. Build the notification document and facilitation workflow; run a structured pilot of 50 defendant contacts before building payment infrastructure.

T4 assumption being tested: defendants presented with an independent EV assessment and a structured buyout pathway will settle at ≥60% before court proceedings.

If FAIL: the double-signal mechanism (C2) does not work at the assumed settlement rate. FMOS falls below 60%. Return to C2 architecture — examine whether a different settlement facilitation design or buyout structure achieves a higher settlement rate.

Dependency: requires Part1 (ITP channel) to deliver the notification.

3

WP2 — independent EV assessment system (assessment org pilot) T4 — high risk

Rationale: risk-first. EV assessment accuracy is the evidential foundation of the double-signal mechanism. Validate with a structured pilot of 20–30 live assessments before building CP2 (which depends on a credible EV figure) or the exchange platform.

T4 assumption being tested: independent assessment organisations (university law departments operating on Calmly's methodology) can produce EV assessments within ±20% of expert median in ≤5 working days at a cost of ≤£16.28/claim.

If FAIL: either accuracy is below threshold (C1 architecture broken) or cost is above ARM ceiling (FMOS broken). Both are architectural failures requiring return to C3 architecture (assessment org design).
4

CP1 + WP1 — claim intake, eligibility assessment, and EV disclosure flow T4 — moderate risk

Rationale: dependency on WP2 (cannot show a credible EV figure without the assessment system). The T4 assumption is the pre-screen pass rate (65%) and the post-EV proceed rate (80%). Both are testable in a Stage 1 pilot but are not structurally architectural — a lower proceed rate adjusts FMOS but does not break the core mechanism.

T4 assumption being tested: 65% of submitted claims pass pre-screen eligibility; 80% of claimants who receive a full EV disclosure proceed to pool matching.

If FAIL (proceed rate significantly below 80%): FMOS deteriorates. Examine whether the CP2 disclosure conversation is the intervention point before adjusting architectural assumptions.
5

Part2 — pool operator onboarding and quality framework T3 — moderate risk

Rationale: dependency on WP3 (pool matching requires at least one live pool operator). The T3 assumption is that pool operators will accept claims on Calmly's terms (including the EV figure as the basis for pool price) and maintain quality standards.

T3 assumption being tested: at least one pool operator agrees to accept matched claims at the Calmly exchange terms and to operate within Calmly's pool quality standards.

If FAIL: the C3 exchange architecture (Calmly bears no working capital) is unvalidated. Redesign the operator economics or revert toward the v1 claims-purchase model — both are architectural changes.
6

WP3 — pool matching algorithm T2

Rationale: dependency on Part2 (pool matching requires pool operators) and WP2 (matching uses the EV figure as primary matching signal). Low assumption uncertainty — matching algorithms are well-understood. Design complexity is in the matching criteria (claim category, EV range, court jurisdiction).

T2 assumption being tested: pool operators can be matched to claims using claim category, EV figure, and court jurisdiction as primary signals — and will accept ≥90% of correctly matched claims.

7

PP1 + PP2 — claimant and defendant fee collection T2–T3

Rationale: dependency on CP3 (defendant fee triggers at D3) and the settlement waterfall. Payment infrastructure is well-understood product territory. The T3 assumption is that claimants are willing to pay 15–25% of recovered amount as a platform fee.

T3 assumption being tested: claimants accept the platform fee structure at sign-up and complete payment at settlement without high churn at the payment step.

8

Ops4 (assessment org pool QA) + Ops5 (ITP outcomes reporting) T2–T3

Rationale: dependency on a live assessment org pool (Ops4) and at least one live ITP partnership (Ops5). Both are HQ operational functions that can be built in parallel with the Stage 1 pilot. Ops5 (ITP outcomes reporting) is the C8 lock-in mechanism — moderate risk because the T3 assumption is that an ITP will actively cite Calmly data in its own reporting.

9

Ops2 — EV methodology and data infrastructure (C10) — full build T2

Rationale: dependency on live claim volume — the data flywheel is only valuable once predictions and outcomes can be compared at scale. Basic data capture (prediction + outcome + metadata) should be built as part of the exchange platform from launch. The analytics layer (model calibration updates, accuracy tracking) is a Stage 2 build requiring ≥500 completed claims for statistically meaningful calibration.

T2 assumption being tested: EV accuracy improves measurably with cumulative volume — the calibration advantage over a new entrant widens with each year of operation.
